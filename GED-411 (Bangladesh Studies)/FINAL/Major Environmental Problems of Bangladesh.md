## 1. Introduction: Environmental Context of Bangladesh

### тЬж English

Bangladesh is one of the most environmentally vulnerable countries in the world due to its unique geographical location, high population density, and dependence on natural resources. The country lies in the delta of major rivers such as the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna, which makes it fertile but also highly exposed to environmental degradation. Rapid population growth, unplanned urbanization, industrialization, and climate change have intensified environmental problems across the country.

Environmental problems refer to conditions that harm ecosystems, human health, and sustainable development. In Bangladesh, these problems are not isolated; rather, they are interconnected and directly affect economic growth, public health, and quality of life.

## 2. Water Pollution

### тЬж English

Water pollution is one of the most severe environmental problems in Bangladesh. Rivers, canals, and wetlands are increasingly polluted by industrial waste, untreated sewage, agricultural runoff, and household waste. Major rivers around Dhaka, such as the Buriganga, Turag, and Shitalakkhya, have become heavily contaminated.

Water pollution affects drinking water supply, aquatic biodiversity, and public health. Waterborne diseases such as diarrhea, cholera, and dysentery are common, especially in rural and slum areas.

**Solution perspective:**
Strict enforcement of environmental laws, establishment of effluent treatment plants (ETPs), proper sewage systems, and public awareness are essential to control water pollution.

## 3. Air Pollution

### тЬж English

Air pollution has become a critical issue, particularly in urban areas such as Dhaka and Chattogram. Major sources of air pollution include vehicle emissions, brick kilns, industrial smoke, construction dust, and burning of fossil fuels.

Air pollution causes respiratory diseases, heart problems, and premature deaths. Children, elderly people, and industrial workers are the most affected groups.

**Solution perspective:**
Use of clean energy, modernization of brick kilns, regulation of vehicle emissions, and expansion of green spaces are necessary to reduce air pollution.

## 4. River Erosion

### тЬж English

River erosion is a major environmental and socio-economic problem in Bangladesh. Every year, thousands of hectares of land are eroded by rivers, displacing millions of people. The erosion-prone rivers include the Jamuna, Padma, and Meghna.

River erosion leads to loss of agricultural land, homelessness, poverty, and internal migration. It creates environmental refugees and increases pressure on urban areas.

**Solution perspective:**
River training projects, embankment construction, dredging, and resettlement programs are required to address river erosion.

## 5. Deforestation and Loss of Biodiversity

### тЬж English

Deforestation is another major environmental problem in Bangladesh. Forests are being destroyed due to illegal logging, population pressure, agricultural expansion, and infrastructure development. As a result, biodiversity is declining rapidly.

**Solution perspective:**
Afforestation programs, community-based forest management, and strict enforcement of forest laws are necessary to conserve biodiversity.
