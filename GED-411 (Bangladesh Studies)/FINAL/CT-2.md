## 1. Introduction to Ethnic Minority People of Bangladesh

### тЬж English

Bangladesh is widely known as a culturally homogeneous country, with the Bengali population forming the majority. However, alongside this majority, Bangladesh is also home to a number of **ethnic minority and indigenous communities**, who possess distinct cultural identities, languages, traditions, and social systems. These communities are commonly referred to as **ethnic minorities**, **indigenous peoples**, or **Adivasis**.

Ethnic minority groups in Bangladesh are mainly concentrated in the **Chittagong Hill Tracts**, **Sylhet region**, **Mymensingh**, **Rajshahi**, and **northern districts**. Despite their rich cultural heritage, these groups often face social, economic, and political marginalization. Understanding their culture and challenges is essential for inclusive national development.

### тЬж English

Among the major indigenous communities of Bangladesh are the **Chakma**, **Marma**, **Garo**, **Santal**, **Khasi**, **Manipuri**, and **Tripura** peoples. Each of these communities maintains a distinct cultural identity shaped by history, geography, and tradition.

The **Chakma**, the largest indigenous group, mainly live in the Chittagong Hill Tracts and follow Buddhism. They have a well-defined social structure and celebrate festivals such as *Bizu*. The **Marma** community, also largely Buddhist, has strong cultural ties with Southeast Asia. The **Garo**, who mainly live in Mymensingh, follow a matrilineal social system, where property is inherited through the female line.

The **Santal** community, one of the largest indigenous groups in northern Bangladesh, traditionally depends on agriculture and follows animistic religious beliefs. The **Khasi** people of Sylhet live in matrilineal societies and practice unique marriage and inheritance customs. These communities speak their own languages, many of which belong to Tibeto-Burman or Austroasiatic language families.

## 3. Cultural Practices, Social Structure, and Language

### тЬж English

Ethnic minority communities of Bangladesh possess rich cultural traditions reflected in their festivals, dress, music, and religious practices. Many indigenous festivals are closely connected with agricultural cycles, such as harvesting and sowing seasons. Traditional dress and ornaments symbolize community identity and social status.

Social structures vary among communities. While some follow patriarchal systems, others, such as the Garo and Khasi, practice matrilineal inheritance. Linguistically, many ethnic groups speak indigenous languages that are different from Bangla. However, due to modernization and lack of institutional support, many of these languages are now endangered.

### **Analyze the socio-economic challenges faced by ethnic minority groups in Bangladesh.**

### тЬж English

Ethnic minority groups in Bangladesh face numerous socio-economic challenges. One of the major issues is **land rights**, as many indigenous people lose ancestral lands due to development projects and lack of legal recognition. In education, language barriers and poverty result in high dropout rates. Access to healthcare is also limited due to geographic isolation and lack of culturally sensitive services.
