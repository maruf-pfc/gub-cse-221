## 1. Introduction to the Economy of Bangladesh

### тЬж English

The economy of Bangladesh has undergone a significant transformation since independence in 1971. Once described as a тАЬbottomless basket,тАЭ the country has emerged as one of the fastest-growing economies in South Asia. Bangladesh now follows a **mixed economic system**, where both public and private sectors play an active role in economic development. The steady growth of GDP, expansion of export-oriented industries, and rising remittance inflows have contributed to this progress.

Despite remarkable achievements, the economy still faces structural challenges such as poverty, inequality, unemployment, and vulnerability to climate change. Understanding the major sectors, recent development trends, and future prospects is essential for analyzing BangladeshтАЩs development path.

### тЬж English

The economy of Bangladesh is mainly driven by **three key sectors**: agriculture, industry, and services.

The **agricultural sector** employs a large portion of the population and ensures food security. Although its contribution to GDP has declined over time, agriculture remains crucial for rural livelihoods.

The **industrial sector**, particularly the **ready-made garments (RMG) industry**, is the backbone of BangladeshтАЩs export earnings. The RMG sector employs millions of workers, especially women, and plays a vital role in poverty reduction.

The **service sector** has expanded rapidly in recent years, including banking, telecommunications, transport, education, and information technology. Additionally, **foreign remittances** from migrant workers significantly contribute to national income and foreign exchange reserves.
