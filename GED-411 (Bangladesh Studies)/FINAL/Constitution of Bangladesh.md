## 1. Meaning and Concept of Constitution

### ‚ú¶ English

A **constitution** is the supreme law of a country which lays down the fundamental principles, structure, powers, and functions of the government. It defines the relationship between the state and its citizens and establishes the basic framework within which political authority is exercised. All laws, policies, and actions of the state must conform to the constitution.

In modern democratic states, the constitution serves not only as a legal document but also as a reflection of national ideology, historical struggles, and collective aspirations of the people. Any law inconsistent with the constitution is considered void.

## 2. Types of Constitution

### ‚ú¶ English

Constitutions can be classified into different types:

1. **Written and Unwritten Constitution**
   A written constitution is codified in a single document, while an unwritten constitution is based on conventions, statutes, and judicial decisions.

2. **Rigid and Flexible Constitution**
   A rigid constitution requires a special procedure for amendment, whereas a flexible constitution can be amended like ordinary laws.

3. **Federal and Unitary Constitution**
   A federal constitution divides power between central and regional governments, while a unitary constitution concentrates power at the center.

Bangladesh has a **written, rigid, and unitary constitution**.

## 3. Historical Background of the Constitution of Bangladesh

### ‚ú¶ English

The Constitution of Bangladesh was framed in the aftermath of the **Liberation War of 1971**, which was fought to establish political freedom, economic justice, and cultural identity. After independence, a Constituent Assembly was formed, and the constitution was adopted on **4 November 1972**. It came into effect on **16 December 1972**, commemorating Victory Day.

The constitution embodies the ideals of the liberation struggle and reflects the desire to establish a democratic, secular, and socialist state.

## 4. Preamble of the Constitution

### ‚ú¶ English

The **Preamble** is the soul of the Constitution of Bangladesh. It declares that all power belongs to the people and pledges to establish a society free from exploitation. It enshrines four fundamental state principles: **nationalism, socialism, democracy, and secularism**.

Though not enforceable by courts, the Preamble plays a crucial role in interpreting constitutional provisions and understanding the spirit of the constitution.

## 5. Salient Features of the Constitution of Bangladesh

### ‚ú¶ English

The Constitution of Bangladesh possesses several distinctive features:

* **Popular Sovereignty** ‚Äì Article 7 declares that all powers belong to the people.
* **Unitary State** ‚Äì Power is centralized.
* **Parliamentary Democracy** ‚Äì Executive authority rests with the Prime Minister.
* **Fundamental Rights** ‚Äì Guaranteed under Part III.
* **Rule of Law** ‚Äì Equality before law is ensured.
* **Judicial Review** ‚Äì Courts can invalidate unconstitutional laws.

These features collectively establish Bangladesh as a democratic constitutional state.

## 6. Fundamental Rights (Part III)

### ‚ú¶ English

Fundamental rights are essential for protecting individual liberty and dignity. The Constitution guarantees rights such as equality before law, freedom of speech, freedom of movement, freedom of religion, and protection of life and personal liberty.

However, these rights are subject to reasonable restrictions in the interest of public order, morality, and national security.

## 8. Amendments of the Constitution

### ‚ú¶ English

Article 142 provides the procedure for constitutional amendments. Bangladesh has experienced frequent amendments, some strengthening democracy (e.g., 12th Amendment), while others weakened it (e.g., 4th Amendment). The judiciary has played a crucial role in reviewing amendments.
